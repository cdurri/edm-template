New data from first H2H RCT of glargine U300 vs degludec U100 in insulin naive T2DM View online
In BRIGHT, degludec U100 and glargine U300 in insulin-naive patients with uncontrolled T2DM both demonstrated powerful HbA1c reduction1
• Primary endpoint:
- Effective HbA1c reduction of –1.6% (baseline: 8.6%) with glargine U300, non-inferior vs degludec U100, ITT population.
HbA1c change (LS mean difference vs degludec U100): –0.05%; 95% CI –0.15 to 0.05; P<0.0001 for non-inferiority of glargine U300 vs degludec U100.
• Safety endpoints:
- lower rates and incidence of anytime† hypoglycaemia during the active titration period (weeks 0–12) were observed with glargine U300 vs degludec U100
- comparable incidence and rates of anytime† hypoglycaemia over the 24-week treatment period
- comparable incidence of nocturnal hypoglycaemia during the titration period and 24-week treatment period

Why hypoglycaemia matters
Hypoglycaemia is a highly feared adverse effect of diabetes medication.2
It matters because:
• Non-severe nocturnal hypoglycaemic events reduce sleep quality, and 7 out of 10 lead to daytime tiredness3
• it makes 61% of family members worry about their loved one.4
Hypoglycaemia or a fear of hypoglycaemia may also have negative effects on:
• initiation of insulin therapy5
• effective insulin titration5
• patient confidence6
• treatment experience and compliance7
• glycaemic control.2,5-7
BRIGHT study design1
BRIGHT is the first head-to-head randomised controlled clinical study comparing the efficacy and safety of two second-generation basal insulin analogues, offering valuable insight into the similarity in glycaemic control and difference in hypoglycaemia risk associated with glargine U300 and degludec U100.
BRIGHT was a 24-week randomised, multicentre, open-label non-inferiority study following 929 insulin-naïve patients with T2DM uncontrolled by OADs with or without GLP-1 receptor agonists. Patients from 16 countries (158 study sites) were randomised 1:1 to receive an evening dose of glargine U300 (n=466) or degludec U100 (n=463). Achievement of the fasting self-monitored plasma glucose target was aimed for during the active titration period (weeks 0–12), but dose titrations were still allowed thereafter.
CI, confidence interval; GLP-1, glucagon-like peptide-1; H2H, head-to-head; ITT, intent-to-treat; LS, least-squares; OAD, oral anti-hyperglycaemic drug; RR, rate ratio; T2DM, type 2 diabetes mellitus.
