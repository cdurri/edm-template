Lowering hypoglycaemic risk for your patients with T2DM with TOUJEO (insulin glargine 300U/mL) vs insulin glargine 100U/mL View online
What we already know

The benefits of TOUJEO have already been established in comparison with a first-generation basal insulin analogue:
In comparison with LANTUS, in patients with T2DM TOUJEO provided:
•Comparable efficacy1,5
HbA1c reductions at 6 months (the primary endpoint) were non-inferior
•Sustained HbA1c reductions over 12 months6
Least squares (LS) mean change in HbA1c from baseline to 12 months 0.10% (95% CI: -0.18 to -0.02)
•Lower hypoglycaemic risk over 12 months6
15% reduction in patients experiencing ≥1 nocturnal events
(Relative Risk 0.85; 95% CI: 0.77 to 0.92)
6% reduction in patients experiencing ≥1 events at any time of day
(Relative Risk 0.94; 95% CI 0.90 to 0.98)

Adapted from: Ritzel R et al, 2018.6
Study design: Patient-level meta-analysis, using data from the EDITION 1, 2 and 3 studies – multicentre, randomised, open-label, two-arm, parallel group, treat-to-target phase IIIa studies of TOUJEO vs LANTUS in adult patients with T2DM. The studies included a 6-month treatment period and a 6-month extension period, and included 2496 participants, of whom 1247 were randomised to TOUJEO and 1249 were randomized to LANTUS.
What’s new?
The BRIGHT study has now established benefits for TOUJEO in comparison with another second-generation basasl insulin analogue.
In BRIGHT, degludec U100 and glargine U300 in insulin-naive patients with uncontrolled T2DM both demonstrated powerful HbA1c reduction7.
• Primary endpoint:
- Effective HbA1c reduction of –1.6% with glargine U300, non-inferior vs degludec U100, ITT population.
HbA1c change (LS mean difference vs U100 degludec): –0.05%; 95% CI –0.15 to 0.05; P<0.0001 for non-inferiority of glargine U300 vs degludec U100.
• Safety endpoints:
- lower rates and incidence of anytime† hypoglycaemia during the active titration period (weeks 0–12) were observed with glargine U300 vs degludec U100
- comparable incidence and rates of anytime† hypoglycaemia over the 24-week treatment period
- comparable incidence of nocturnal hypoglycaemia during the titration period and 24-week treatment period
†Anytime hypoglycaemia: hypoglycaemia experienced during a 24-hour period.

Study design: BRIGHT was a 24-weeek randomised, multicentre, openlabel inferiority study following 929 insulin- naïve patients with T2DM uncontrolled by OADs with or without GLP-1 receptor agonists. Patients were randomised 1:1 to receive an evening does of Toujeo 300 U/mL (n=466) or insulin degludec 100 U/mL (n=463)
Why prevent hypos with a second-generation basal insulin analogue?
Hypoglycaemia or a fear of hypoglycaemia may have negative effects on:
•initiation of insulin therapy8
•effective insulin titration8
•patient confidence9
•treatment experience and compliance10
•glycaemic control.8–11
CI, confidence interval; GLP-1, glucagon-like peptide-1; OAD, oral anti-hyperglycaemic drug; SE, standard error; T2DM, type 2 diabetes mellitus.
